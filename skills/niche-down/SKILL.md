# Niche Down

**Command:** `/niche-down`

**From:** $100M Offers by Alex Hormozi

**When to use:** Choosing who to sell to, escaping a crowded market, or when your offer isn't converting and you need to reposition.

## What it does

Helps you find the most profitable, specific market for your product using Hormozi's market selection framework — because the market matters MORE than your offer or your skills.

## The Core Principle

> "Starving Crowd (market) > Offer Strength > Persuasion Skills"

If you have a starving crowd and a mediocre offer, you win. If you have a great offer and the wrong market, you lose.

## The Three Market Criteria

Hormozi identifies three "hot" markets where money flows:

1. **Health** — people want to feel good and live longer
2. **Wealth** — people want to make more money
3. **Relationships** — people want love, connection, or status

Every successful niche lives inside one of these. If yours doesn't, you're fighting uphill.

## The Four Market Variables

When selecting a niche within those markets, look for:

| Variable | What it means |
|----------|--------------|
| **Massive pain** | They are bleeding. Urgent, not just inconvenient. |
| **Purchasing power** | They can actually afford your solution |
| **Easy to target** | You can find them (communities, platforms, events, ads) |
| **Growing market** | The wave is coming in, not going out |

> "If you find a market that has all four, go for it. The narrower you go, the less competition you face, and the higher the price you can charge."

## The Niching Down Example (from the book)

- General: "I help people lose weight" → crowded, commodity pricing
- Better: "I help people over 40 lose weight" → less crowded
- Better: "I help women over 40 lose weight" → even less crowded
- Best: "I help female executives over 40 lose weight without giving up wine" → premium pricing, specific audience, easy to target

Each step down the niche = less competition = higher prices = easier marketing.

## The Commitment Rule

> "Commit to the niche. Do not waver. Many people switch before they see results. The switching is what kills them."

Pick one niche. Run it for 6+ months. Let compounding work.

## Prompt template

```
My product/service: [describe it]
Current target: [who I'm selling to now]
Current price: $[X]
Problem I solve: [1 sentence]

Apply the Hormozi niche-down framework:
1. Which of the 3 hot markets (health/wealth/relationships) does this fit?
2. List 5 more specific sub-niches within my current target
3. Score each on: pain level, purchasing power, targetability, market growth (1-10 each)
4. Recommend the highest-scoring niche
5. Show me 3 levels of niching down with example positioning statements
6. Rewrite my offer headline for the recommended niche
```

## Key principle

> "The pain itself is not enough. They need to know they have a problem, know it can be solved, and believe YOU are the one to solve it."
> — Alex Hormozi, $100M Offers
